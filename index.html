TEC-Approved PQC Algorithms for Indian Infrastructure

üáÆüá≥ TEC India PQC Approval Status

The following algorithms have been evaluated and approved by TEC India for use in critical telecommunications and digital infrastructure across India, aligned with international standards and national security requirements.

ML-KEM (CRYSTALS-Kyber)

Type: Key Encapsulation Mechanism

TEC Status: Approved for Critical Infrastructure

Foundation: Module Learning With Errors

Parameters: ML-KEM-512, ML-KEM-768, ML-KEM-1024

Use Cases: 5G networks, Banking systems, Government communications

TEC Approved

ML-DSA (CRYSTALS-Dilithium)

Type: Digital Signature Algorithm

TEC Status: Approved for All Applications

Foundation: Module Learning With Errors

Parameters: ML-DSA-44, ML-DSA-65, ML-DSA-87

Use Cases: Digital identity, PKI systems, Document signing

TEC Approved

SLH-DSA (SPHINCS+)

Type: Stateless Hash-based Digital Signature

TEC Status: Approved for High-Security Applications

Foundation: Hash Functions

Special Features: Mathematical diversity, Conservative security

Use Cases: Defense systems, Long-term archives, Critical certificates

TEC Approved

HQC

Type: Key Encapsulation Mechanism

TEC Status: Under Evaluation (Conditional Approval)

Foundation: Quasi-Cyclic Codes

Purpose: Backup to ML-KEM with different mathematics

Expected: Full approval by 2027

Evaluation Phase

FALCON

Type: Digital Signature Algorithm

TEC Status: Provisional Approval for Testing

Foundation: NTRU Lattices

Advantage: Compact signatures, Efficient verification

Use Cases: IoT devices, Mobile applications, Constrained environments

Testing Phase

üìã TEC India Implementation Guidelines

  • Primary Algorithms: ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA are fully approved for immediate deployment
  • Hybrid Transition: TEC recommends hybrid classical-PQC implementations during transition period
  • Critical Infrastructure: Defense and banking systems must implement multiple algorithm types
  • Compliance Timeline: Government systems must begin PQC integration by 2025, complete by 2030

TEC India Guidelines & Resources

üìã About TEC MTCTE Regulations

The Mandatory Testing Certification of Telecommunication Equipment (MTCTE) regulations, implemented by TEC under the Department of Telecommunications, require that all telecommunication equipment must undergo mandatory testing and certification before sale, import or use in India.

üèõÔ∏è TEC Standards & Essential Requirements

  • General Requirements (GRs): Fundamental specifications for telecommunication equipment quality controls
  • Interface Requirements (IRs): Specifications for equipment interfaces and interoperability
  • Service Requirements (SRs): Service-specific technical requirements and standards
  • EMI/EMC Requirements: Electromagnetic interference and compatibility standards
  • Safety Requirements: Equipment safety standards for user and network protection
  • Technical Requirements: Wireless, IPv4, IPv6, optical parameters, and other technical specifications
  • TEC GR No.: 91000:2022: Quantum Key Distribution System standards

üáÆüá≥ Regulatory Framework & Compliance

  • Indian Telegraph Act, 1885: Legal foundation for telecommunication equipment regulation
  • MTCTE Regulations 2017: Mandatory certification requirements implemented since 2019
  • NCIIPC Guidelines: National Critical Information Infrastructure Protection Centre requirements
  • CERT-In Framework: Indian Computer Emergency Response Team cybersecurity standards
  • RBI Cybersecurity: Reserve Bank of India guidelines for financial systems
  • DoT Telecommunications Rules 2025: Draft gazette notification for updated standards and certification rules
  • National Quantum Mission: India's flagship quantum technology initiative established through collaboration with NSCS

üß™ TEC Testing & Certification Process

  • Accredited Laboratories: Testing by Indian Accredited labs designated by TEC, IEC/ISO 17025 compliant
  • Certificate Validity: TEC certificates are valid for ten years from date of issue
  • Processing Time: Certification process typically takes 2-3 months
  • Product Marking: Products must be marked with TEC logo and ID number (individual factory code)
  • Indian Representative: Foreign OEMs must appoint authorized Indian representative for certification process
  • Simplified Certification: Available for specific product categories with declaration-based process
  • Factory Inspections: Currently not required but may be implemented in future

üìö TEC Technical Initiatives

  • 3GPP Standards Adoption: TSDSI transposed 3GPP standards for Release 13-17 into national standards
  • 5G/LTE Standards: Adoption of 3GPP Release 10-16 standards for LTE-Advanced corresponding to ITU-R M.2012
  • AI in Telecom: Standards for assessing AI systems fairness and AI-native telecommunication networks
  • IoT Security: Code of Practice for securing consumer Internet of Things (IoT) released by TEC
  • Quantum Technologies: Quantum Random Number Generator standards and QKD system guidelines
  • NGN Transport Lab: TEC NGN Transport Lab for stakeholder testing and validation
  • Free Space Optical: Standards for optical communication in 4G/5G cellular networks and Li-Fi access points

üè¢ TEC International Collaborations

  • IEEE Partnership: TEC has collaborative relationship with IEEE Standards Association (IEEE-SA)
  • IEEE-SA Standards Board: TEC nominees appointed as primary and alternate members on IEEE-SA Standards Board under GEPS program
  • OCEANIS Initiative: TEC is founding member of Forum for Ethics in Standards for Autonomous and Intelligent Systems
  • IEEE Xplore Access: TEC subscription to IEEE digital library with 5+ million articles, standards, and research papers
  • ITU Collaboration: Active participation in ITU-T Study Groups and multilateral telecommunications organizations
  • C-DOT Partnership: Collaboration with Centre for Development of Telematics for quantum key distribution development
  • Global Standards Bodies: Coordination with international standards organizations for next-generation network elements
